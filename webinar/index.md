### Life Sector Analysis

*Adapted from [Lorna Rasmussen's (my mom!) Wheel of Life](http://lornarasmussen.com/) and [Abrah Dresdale's Regenerate Change](https://www.regeneratechange.com/)*

Productivity is all about time management. So the first thing you need to do is determine where you want to spend your time! It starts with a bull's eye...

* Draw out three concentric circles on your paper.
* Draw a horizontal and vertical line through the middle like a cross hair.
* Draw an X through the center so you have a pie with 8 slices.

This is your life sector chart. Each of the 8 sections represent a facet of your life, you get to choose these or use the following examples:

1. Finances
2. Home
3. Love life
4. Family
5. Physical health
6. Career
7. Spiritual life
8. Artistic life

Label each of the pie slices. Remember this is your life, so choose what matters to you. If you have fewer than 8 feel free to combine two or more slices together! Be creative.

Now we will label our three circles starting with the inner one:

* Growing
* Sustaining
* Thriving

These represent how well you are doing in each of your sectors. The closer to the center the weaker a sector is. The further out the more you feel that you are thriving. Go ahead and fill in each sector based on how well you feel that you are doing in that area of your life.

For bonus points you can add arrows to indicate if something is improving or slipping. Again be creative with this! Once it's done hang it up somewhere important so you can get a good look at it each day.

### Kanban

*Adapted from [Agile software development](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Agile_software_development) and [Agile Learning Centers](https://agilelearningcenters.org/)*

Kanban is a Japanese word meaning "sticky board". It's simply a board with sticky notes on it. So you'll need a board of some kind or even a wall along with some sticky notes.

On your board draw 4 columns each with a label at the top. They will each be different sizes starting from the left:

1. Could Do (backlog) - make this column the largest, about 50%.
2. Will Do (Ready) - about 20%
3. Doing - this column should be the smallest, only the width of a sticky note or about 10%
4. Done - the final column about 20%

This is just the start of the Kanban, you'll adapt it later on based on your workflow needs.

Now grab your to-do list (or combine all the random to-do lists you have laying around). Start to write out your to-dos on sticky notes and place them in the **could do** column. Once that's done start to prioritize the tasks you are going to do today or this week and move them to the **will do** column.

This is the first pattern of Kanban, limiting your Work In Progress or WIP. You've taken your big long to-do list and prioritized the items into a smaller more manageable list!

You could add more columns for **this month, this week, and today** to further break up your to-do items.

Now when you are working on a task you move it into doing. It's important to only do one task at a time! This is the second pattern of Kanban; bringing focus to your work. If you are working on something and realize you need to do some other task you are able to add it to the Kanban and then forget it and return to the task you are currently doing, rather than get side tracked!

Finally once you've completed a task you move it to done. Now you have a record of what you've got done. This is the third pattern; cycles of intention setting, doing, and reflecting. By visiting the Kanban on a regular basis you can begin to set better intentions (moving tasks from **could do** to **will do**).

### Personal Mastery Board

*Adapted from [Agile Learning Centers' Community Mastery Board](https://agilelearningcenters.org/)*

Building habits is challenging. This tool helps you to become aware of problems or opportunities and gracefully apply incremental solutions. It's just an adapted Kanban board, so you just need stickies and (optionally) a wall or board.

Start by creating four columns:

1. Awareness
2. Implementation
3. Practicing
4. Mastery

Once you've got the board setup it's time to set your rhythm. Pull out your calendar and set a recurring event called "personal mastery". I chose to do it every week on Sunday, but you can pick any cadence that works for you.

To start, bring your attention to the **awareness** column by writing out things that you are aware of in your life. These can be feelings (lower back pain, tiredness, loneliness) or behaviors (indulging addictions like smoking or drinking, staying up late, spending too much money). Basically anything you want to address or change. Write a few of these out on stickies and put them in the **awareness** column.

Reflect on these awarnesses and choose one to formulate a solution for. Perhaps you are aware that you stay up late and you'd like to stop doing that! You will **implement** a solution to **try out**. I notice that looking at screens late at night keeps me awake so I might implement a rule "no screens past 9pm". I'll write that out on a sticky and attach it to the original awareness then move them both to the **implementation** column.

You job between rhythms is to try out these solutions. If next time I return to the board and notice that my implementation didn't stick I can reevaluate it. Perhaps it's not something I want to change or the solution isn't the right one. If it doesn't work I might try to implement something else or just move the original awareness back and pick something new. If a solution *is working* then I'll move it to the **practicing** column.

Practice has two relevant meanings. It means to habitually **do** something and it refers to the exercise of doing something to reach proficiency. You are both bringing a solution into your practice and getting better at doing it.

Finally a solution can move into the **mastery** column once you do it as second nature.

This tool helps you track the things you want to do and why you want to do them. It also helps frame solutions as experiments to work towards.
